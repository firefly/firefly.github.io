Our Story

Firefly was born in April 2017 when RicMoo (Richard Moore) and YuetLoo created a hardware wallet to store Richard's prized crypto kitty as their entry for a Blockgeeks Lab Blockchain Hackathon. The team placed second at the hackathon, and went on to continue building the Firefly hardware wallet.

Richard is the founder and maintainer of Ethers JS, a library to help developers build on Ethereum that is downloaded millions of times per week. Ethers grew in popularity from 2018 to today leaving little time to dedicate to Firefly. However in 2022, with Ethers V6 released and account abstraction on the horizon, the timing felt perfect to revive Firefly.

In the six months from August 2022 to January 2023, the Firelfy Pixie underwent several iterations, from Rev 2 to to Rev 6 ‚Äî which continues to be the version used in Firefly DevKits.

We're currently working on hardware wallet firmware for the Firefly Pixie and developer tools to make it easier to build and customize Firefly firmware.

In July 2023 the team attended ETHCC 7 in Brussels. It was the first time we were able to get Firefly devices into the hands of developers. As well as Brussels, the Firefly team has attended DevCon in Bangkok and ETHCC 8 in Cannes. We plan to give away the most Firefly DevKits yet at DevConnect in Buenos Aires in November this year.

The Firefly Pixie DevKit is our first release. Devices and firmware can be modified or independently developed. Everything you need is on our GitHub. If you have questions or want to get involved, visit the forum or say hi in our Telegram group.
